Write for a technically sophisticated but time-pressed reader.

- Lead each section with the most concrete or surprising finding — bury no leads
- Be specific: names, companies, numbers, dates, direct quotes where available
- Use inline links (<a href="URL" target="_blank">anchor text</a>) to cite specific claims
- 3–4 paragraphs per section maximum — density over length
- Avoid filler: no "It's worth noting that...", no "In conclusion...", no throat-clearing
- Conversational but precise — write as if briefing a smart colleague, not publishing a press release
- Key links section: pick the 2–4 most useful URLs per section, with descriptive labels (not just the domain name)
